On screen & on stage

Actress

Sara Sommerfeld has been a working actor in film, television and theatre since 1989. A graduate of the Swedish Theatre Academy, she has built a career spanning more than three decades and over 50 productions — establishing herself as one of Sweden's most versatile performers.

Most recently she performed the critically acclaimed solo show Show Bizniz on Dramaten's main stage, broadcast on SVT in 2025. Known for her ability to move fluidly between genres — from drama to comedy, arthouse to mainstream — she continues to take on work that challenges and surprises.

Behind the camera

Director

Sara Sommerfeld is educated in directing and screenwriting at Stockholm University of Dramatic Arts, and is a graduate of the highly acclaimed screenwriting school Alma Manusutbildning. Her films consistently center on human stories told with intimacy and social awareness.

She is currently developing the dramedy TV series Beas Bröllop.

2015 · TV4 · Documentary

My Grandmother and the Holocaust

Broadcast in prime time on TV4 in May 2015. The film received the prestigious scholarship from the Micael Bindefeld Foundation in memory of the Holocaust, and was TV4's contribution to the Kristallen television awards.

2020 · SVT · Short Film

När själen darrar

Screened on SVT in 2020. The film follows a young Syrian man — a trained engineer — who chooses to pursue his dream of becoming an actor. A story about following one's calling against all odds.

In print

Author

With the same unflinching honesty she brings to the stage, Sara Sommerfeld writes autobiographical novels that blend self-inquiry with warmth and humor. Her debut sold 20,000 copies in its first year and earned a 5/5 rating from BTJ — described as "brilliant." She was nominated for the Adlibris Book Award for Debut of the Year and was a finalist for Best Feelgood 2025.

Debut Novel · 2024

Is Everything I Learned About Love a Lie?

Sara Sommerfeld unabashedly faces her self-hatred, doubts and fragile self-esteem — and travels to Bali to find herself. With Elizabeth Gilbert's Eat, Pray, Love as a companion, it becomes both a dramatic and life-changing journey. A novel about traveling far away to find home.
